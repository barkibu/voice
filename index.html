Privacy

What leaves your Mac, and what doesn't

Auto-submit only sends the words you explicitly add to the dictionary. Your audio, your transcripts, and your notes stay local. Mark any entry as private if you don't want it shared.

Sent to Supabase on auto-submit

  • The word + replacement you typed
  • The matching threshold (a number)
  • The category (breed, claim, market…)
  • Your @barkibu.com email
  • The app version

Never sent. Anywhere.

  • Audio recordings
  • Dictation transcripts
  • Other words from your dictionary
  • Anything marked 🔒 private
  • Hardware identifiers

FAQ

Common questions

Why a fork instead of Wispr Flow?
Wispr Flow is paid per seat. Across the Barkibu team that adds up. Barkibu Voice is built on top of Muesli (open source, MIT) and runs every transcription on the device — no per-minute API cost, no recurring license. Plus we control the dictionary.
Will my Spanish dictation get translated to English?
No. The Whisper backend gets a language hint from Settings → Dictation language. Pick Spanish (or any of the 13 markets) and decoding is locked to that language. The "spoken Spanish, transcribed English" bug some of us saw on Parakeet is fixed by switching to Whisper Large Turbo with the language hint.
Can I keep some words private?
Yes. Each entry in your dictionary has a 🔒 toggle. Flip it on, and that word stays on your Mac, never gets auto-submitted to the team queue. Useful for client names, internal codenames, or anything you don't want propagated.
What if I don't sign in?
The app still works as a local dictation tool. You just don't get the community dictionary, and the words you add stay on your Mac. Sign in any time later from Settings.
Where do approved words live?
In a Supabase project owned by Barkibu (eu-west-3). Reads are scoped to @barkibu.com JWTs via row-level security. Writes to the approved dictionary go through Pablo or Álvaro reviewing the queue in Supabase Studio. Nobody outside Barkibu has access.
Does it work on Intel Macs?
It builds and runs, but the on-device transcription model is optimized for the Apple Neural Engine. On Intel it falls back to CPU and you'll feel the latency. Apple Silicon (M1 and up) is what we target.
How do I add a new word to the team?
Don't think about it. Just add it to your personal dictionary like you would normally, and the auto-submit hook proposes it to the review queue. If approved, it lands on every other Mac on the next sync (≤30 minutes).
